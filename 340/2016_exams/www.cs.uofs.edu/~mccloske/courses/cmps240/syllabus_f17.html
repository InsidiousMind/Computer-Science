Programming Assignments: 

There will be approximately eight to ten programming assignments. Grades will be based upon not only correctness and efficiency but also on subjective attributes such as quality of documentation, proper code formatting, and readability.

Submitted solutions must include comments that identify the student(s) submitting it (e.g., Chris Smith), the course and semester (in this case, CMPS 240, Fall 2017), the assignment number (e.g., Prog. Assg. #2), and a list of names of any persons who provided assistance in completing the work. They should also include comments that point out any known flaws in the work.

Work submitted after the deadline is subject to a penalty of ten percent per day. As a general rule, work cannot be submitted more than 72 hours past the time that it is due.

Students are allowed to collaborate with each other while working on assignments, but such collaboration is to be acknowledged, in writing, within the submitted work, as mentioned above. Collaboration should not go so far that it becomes untruthful for a student to claim that submitted work is, for the most part, his or her own (or that of his/her team, in the case of a team project). Under no circumstances is a student to copy the work of another or to allow another student to copy her/his work.

Intended Course Outcomes

Upon completion of the course, a successful student will

  • be capable of developing object-oriented software that makes proper use of generics, inheritance, and polymorphism.
  • appreciate the utility of, and know when to use, several standard abstract data types, including stacks, queues, lists, trees, and graphs.
  • be capable of devising interfaces and implementations of abstract data types appropriate for solving a given problem.
  • be capable of analyzing a program (involving loops and/or recursion) in order to determine its asymptotic running time and space usage.
  • fully comprehend several classic algorithms, including ones for searching, sorting, data compression, and traversing graphs.
